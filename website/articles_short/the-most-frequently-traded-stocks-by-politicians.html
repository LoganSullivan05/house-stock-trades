The Most Frequently Traded Stocks by Politicians

Introduction

Congressional stock disclosures reveal certain companies that consistently draw interest from lawmakers. This article highlights the most frequently traded stocks by members of Congress, and what it may suggest about policy and perception.

Big Tech Dominance

Stocks like Apple (AAPL), Microsoft (MSFT), Amazon (AMZN), and Alphabet (GOOGL) top the list. Their growth potential and central role in modern infrastructure make them popular choices across party lines.

Healthcare and Biotech

Pfizer (PFE), Johnson & Johnson (JNJ), and Moderna (MRNA) are frequently traded. These companies are often involved in federal research or pandemic response, making their stock performance closely tied to legislation.

Defense and Aerospace

Defense contractors like Lockheed Martin (LMT) and Raytheon Technologies (RTX) are commonly held. Lawmakers on defense or appropriations committees are particularly likely to trade in this sector.

Financial Giants

Stocks such as JPMorgan Chase (JPM), Bank of America (BAC), and Goldman Sachs (GS) appear frequently in disclosures, possibly reflecting interest in the broader economy and market stability.
